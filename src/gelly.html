Gelly

Designing mid-air interactions with a proximity sensor

This project is funded by Qualcomm.

Problem

Gelly is a flexible and transparent mutual capacitance sensor that currently has proximity and touch sensing capabilities. My project aims to explore the application space of this sensor.

Solution

My research shows that proximity sensors are widely explored in phones but in-air gestures would be highly beneficial in solving the real-estate problems that small screened wearables such as the smartwatch is currently experiencing.

Additionally, mid-air interactions could solve problems in situations where people cannot touch a screen for sterility, or cleanliness reasons.

Process

The first step of the project was naturally brainstorming for applications where proximity could be beneficial. My ideation process was coming up with general fields that might be useful to explore, and then thinking deeper on how proximity could be applied to solve existent problems. I used Sketch to design laser-cut wrist-bands for the Gelly sensor to sit on.

Step 2: Setting Focus

To set focus, I did background research to figure out what is an innovative field that Gelly could be used in that could solve people's problems in their daily life. I set focus on mid-air interactions for wearables(to take advantage of the flexibility property of the technology) and to solve problems that people face when they cannot touch the screen of their devices.

Me and my undergraduate student wrote a prior art review from our background research. This documentation involved prior academic research, other technologies out there, and patent search.

I also wrote a Java program to visualize data received from the sensor and started implementing Gelly's software.

After I have a working prototype I will run my user study to get information on what users find good and bad about my interaction techniques.
